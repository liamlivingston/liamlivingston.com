60V Battery v1 and v2

I bought 10 20-cell escooter battery packs for $210 total($1.05 per cell). These cells are EVEICR18650-26V cells which have a capacity of 2550mah and a max discharge rate if 7.65a.

×

I connected these cells in a 10p17s to get a 60v 33ah battery(1560.6wh) with a max discharge rate of 4.7Kw. For v1 of the battery I made it in a triangle shape to fit the frame of my v2.1 ebike.

×

For v2 of the battery I dissasambled v1 of the battery for the cells. I then tested those 170 cells and a few more that i had lying around for a total of about 200 cells. I also replaced any damaged heat shrink on the batteries. I took 170 of the highest capacity cells and entered their capacity data into a tool called rePackr to find the optimal configuation of the cells. This time I decided to make it a rectangle shape because I would use this for my v2.2 ebike and as a range extender for my v3 ebike.

For this v2 of this batttery, I used my new spot-weld-on terminals and also used cell holders to build it rather than hot gluing the cells together and soldering the terminals on top of the nickel strip that was already spot welded on.
