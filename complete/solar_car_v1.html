Overview

I joined PASV (now MSRC) in November 2022 (my sophomore year) as a founding member and was appointed Electrical and Solar Lead.
Due to being the only team member with electrical experiance, I ended up doing almost all of the electrical work.

×

Aux System


The Aux System in this car was relatively limited to the limited amound of time we had.

It was powered off of a LiTime 12v 50ah LiFePO4 battery.

It consisted of lights, fans, and a horn that were all required by the SCC.



×

Main System


The Main System in this car was also relatively limited.

It was powered by 4 LiTime 12v 100ah LiFePO4 batteries wired in series that gave us a 48v (51.2v nominal) 100ah pack with a capacity of 5120Wh

There were 12 100w Renogy solar pannels wired in a 6s2p configuration that was connected to a MidNite Solar Classic 150 MPPT.

The motor on the car was a 3kW BLDC Motor from Golden Motor, powered by a Vector 200 Controller.

Building process

We traveled to Dallas, Texas in July 2023 for the 2023 Cross Country Solar Car Challenge.

We went throught a 3-day scrutineering period where we passed 7 different inspections.

After passing the Electrical Station, I was done with scrutineering because other members were better suited for the other inspections.

I decided to help other teams who were struggling with the Electrical Station for which I was later awarded the Jeff Barrett Solar Car Spirit Award.
